Frequently Asked Questions
About Ma'rifa 2022

Why did this conference start? What is Ma’rifa’s mission?

Ma’rifa is a national organization committed to the spiritual and intellectual growth of Shi’i students. By curating resources, crafting content, and building a community network, we provide students with a framework for critical leadership, grounded in culturally relevant, Islamic perspectives. Our goal is to invest in and enrich the Shi’i student journey in order to discover our individual and collective ma’rifa.

We held our first conference in 2020 at Harvard University. It was the first step towards this goal, spurred by the need for a forum of exploring God-centric knowledge for students, scholars, and community leaders.

Can you expand on this year’s theme, “Heart & Revival”?

Our theme this year is inspired by the following Hadith Qudsi, as translated by Sheikh Mohammad Ali Shomali:

“Neither My earth nor My heaven can contain Me. I am contained only in the heart of My believer.”
We pray that with this theme in mind we are all able to revive the commitment in our hearts to become believers who can truly know and love God and His creation.

When and where is the Ma’rifa 2022 conference going to be held this year?

Ma’rifa 2022 “Heart & Revival” will be held from March 25th to March 27th with our Friday program commencing at 9PM EST and Sunday program ending by 10am EST. The conference will take place at Whittemore House in Washington D.C.

Who are the speakers for this year’s conference?

We are still confirming our speakers and schedule and will release everything on our website and social media as we get closer to the conference.
Registration

Is the conference in-person this year?

It is! It will be held in Whittemore House in Washington, DC on March 25th-27th. We will provide a virtual option for those who are not able to join us in person.

Am I eligible to attend the in-person conference?

You can register for the in-person conference if you meet the following criteria:
  • Undergraduate Shi'i Students in North America (including 2-year, 4-year, technical, and seminary school)
  • If you have no college affiliation due to not enrolling in college/dropping out/recently graduating, then you must be under 22 to attend the conference. Please note that high school students are not eligible to attend Ma'rifa 2022.

Am I eligible to attend the virtual conference?

You can register for the in-person conference if you meet the following criteria:
  • Undergraduate Shi'i Students in North America (including 2-year, 4-year, technical, and seminary school)
  • Recent Alumnus (less than 2 years after undergrad)
  • Graduate Shi'i Students in North America (including PhD students)
  • If you have no college affiliation due to not enrolling in college/dropping out/recently graduating, then you must be under 24 to attend the conference. Please note that high school students are not eligible to attend Ma'rifa 2022.

What are the steps required to register?

To complete your registration, please purchase a ticket AND fill out the relevant registration form.

Where can I register for the conference?

You can register for the in-person conference here: bit.ly/marifa-DC
You can register for the virtual conference here: bit.ly/marifa-virtual

Where can I purchase the tickets?

You can purchase the tickets at the same links as registration.

How many tickets will be available for the conference?

Our in-person conference tickets will be limited to 250 people to ensure Covid safety and the integrity of the venue.
Attendance

What are the steps required to attend the in-person conference?

You must complete your registration, arrange travel and transportation to Washington, D.C, and confirm your lodging.

I am coming from outside the area, where should I stay?

There are many options for you to stay in D.C. You are welcome to stay with family/friends. Ma’rifa will also have a designated hotel walking distance to the venue for you to book at a discounted price soon. Stay tuned for more information about that.
Financial

How much do tickets cost?

For in-person tickets, early bird tickets will be $30 and regular tickets will be $40. All virtual tickets will be $10.

How is the conference being funded?

Ma’rifa Inc. is a registered 501c3 non-profit organization. Our programs are sponsored primarily by academic grants through universities, Muslim organizations, and public donations from community donors, masjids, centers and other religious organizations. If you are interested in supporting our work, please reach out to us at info@marifaprograms.com.

What if it’s too expensive for me?

We have limited funding available for transportation and lodging on a need-basis. Once you have registered and if you anticipate needing financial-aid, please contact us at info@marifaprograms.com. Please note that we strive to make all Ma'rifa programming financially accessible for everyone. Our financial-aid is based on mutual trust and proceeds from the conference will go towards future student programming.

Are tickets refundable in case something comes up and I can’t attend?

We understand that life happens and will try our best to work with you to process a refund if applicable.
Covid-19 Safety Guidelines

Is vaccination required for the in-person conference?

Yes! In order to ensure the safety of all in-person attendees, Ma’rifa will be requiring full vaccination from all attendees. To provide proof of your vaccination, please upload a picture of your vaccination card during online registration

Our definition of "fully vaccinated" is in accordance with the CDC's guidelines: A person is considered fully vaccinated 2 weeks after completion of a two-dose mRNA series or one dose of Janssen vaccine.

Will masks be required at the in-person conference?

Masks will be required for Ma’rifa 2022 for the duration of the conference.

What if I did not get vaccinated?

Please join us virtually by registering at bit.ly.marifa-virtual

What happens if the conference is cancelled because of Covid-19?

In the case that Ma'rifa cancels the conference due to Covid-19 restrictions, we will refund the full ticket amount. However, Ma'rifa is not responsible for any travel or lodging expenses incurred.
Attendance

Will the conference be recorded?

The conference will not be recorded for external use. You must be present in-person or tune in virtually to benefit from the content.

How should I prepare for the conference?

Before the conference, we ask that you respond to our Call for Responses prompt (upcoming). We are a content driven conference so the most important factor in making Ma’rifa 2022 successful will be engagement from our attendees. We ask that you please come ready to participate, meet, organize, and learn with your peers.

Where should I go if I still have more questions?
